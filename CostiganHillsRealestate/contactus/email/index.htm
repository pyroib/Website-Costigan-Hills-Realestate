How To Buy a House.

From time to time prospective buyers find that the house they loved gets sold to another party.
There may make an offer either verbally or in writing to purchase a property in mistaken belief that making an offer gave them some hold over the property, and from the time of making their offer the property would be withdrawn from sale to any purchaser until their offer was dealt with.

An offer to purchase only begomes legal and binding once the contract of sale is signed by the sellers and all allterations are initialed by both the purchaser and the sellers as required under the law of contract.

Although the potential purchaser may have signed a contract of sale and the owners may have verbally accepted the offer, the property is not sold and has not been purchased until the Contract of Sale is signed by all parties.

Sometimes an owner may verbally accept an offer with full intentions of proceeding with the sale. However, if another purchaser places an offer to purchase the property before the owners have signed the contract of sale, the agent must submit by law the offer to the owner for consideration. The owners may then instruct the agent to advise the first purchaser that they have received another offer and ask them if they wish to reveiw the amount they have offered to pay for the property. Sho9uld this happen to you we will advise you in writing that there is another offer and set out in the letter how the offers will be handled.

WE WILL

1/ Submit all offers to the sellers promptly- pending their availability

2/ Comunicate the progress of the offer to the prospective purchasers promptly.

If your offer is accepted you will be advised promptly and given the opportunity to sign the contract with the protection of the 5 day cooling off period and a copy of the countersigned contract will be given to you, or forwarded onto your solicitor/conveyancer as soon as possible after signing by the seller.

IF your offer is not accepted your deposit can only be refunded once your cheque has been cleared through tyhe banking system which takes 3 business days. We cannot give you a cash refund. We are legally bound under The Real estate Act to withdraw monies from our trust Accont in the form of a cheque only.

3/ In summary an offer places the seller under no obligation until they sign the Contract Of Sale.

Remember, a 5 day cooling off period commences from the day the contract is signed by all parties and dated, The purchaser has 5 working days of which they can rescind from the contract for any reason, However you may fofeit 0.25% of the purchase price to the vendor.

Best wishes in buying your new home.
